## What Are Runes?

Runes are more than just letters—they are symbols that carried deep meaning for the Norse, Germanic, and Anglo-Saxon peoples who used them from roughly 150 CE to 800 CE. Each rune represents not just a sound, but also a concept, a force of nature, or a spiritual principle.

The word "rune" itself comes from the Old Norse word *rún*, meaning "secret" or "mystery." And mysterious they are! Each rune was believed to hold magical properties and was used not only for writing but also for divination, protection, and invoking specific energies.

## The Elder Futhark

The Elder Futhark is the oldest form of the runic alphabet, consisting of 24 runes. The name "Futhark" comes from the first six runes: **F**ehu, **U**ruz, **Th**urisaz, **A**nsuz, **R**aidho, and **K**enaz—much like how we call our modern alphabet the "ABC's."

These 24 runes are traditionally divided into three groups of eight, called *aettir* (singular: *aett*), meaning "families" or "clans." Each aett is associated with a Norse deity:

1. **Freyr's Aett** - The first eight runes, associated with fertility, prosperity, and earthly matters
2. **Heimdall's Aett** - The middle eight runes, associated with challenges, transformation, and the elements
3. **Tyr's Aett** - The final eight runes, associated with cosmic order, community, and completion
