# Chapter 3: Uruz (ᚢ) - Strength and Vitality

## The Rune

**Symbol:** ᚢ
**Name:** Uruz (pronounced "OO-rooz")
**Literal Meaning:** Aurochs (wild ox), Primal Strength
**Unicode:** U+16A2
**UTF-8:** E1 9A A2

## Historical Context

Uruz represents the aurochs—a massive, now-extinct wild ox that once roamed the forests of Europe. These creatures stood nearly six feet tall at the shoulder and were formidable beasts: powerful, untamed, and dangerous. The last aurochs died in Poland in 1627, but during the Viking Age, they were still known and feared.

Hunting an aurochs was a rite of passage that proved a warrior's courage and strength. The animal represented raw, primal power—the kind of force that cannot be civilized, only respected.

## Shape and Form

The shape of Uruz (ᚢ) resembles the horns of the aurochs curving downward, or an upside-down horseshoe. It's formed by a single vertical line with a curved or angled line descending from the top, creating an inverted "U" or "n" shape.

Some runologists see it as representing the powerful shoulders and head of the aurochs lowered in a charge—a moment before tremendous force is released.

## Pronunciation Guide

**Name:** Uruz
**Sound:** "U" as in "moon" or "oo" as in "food"

This rune represented the /u/ sound in Old Norse, similar to the "oo" in "boot." When speaking the rune's name, emphasize the first syllable: "OO-rooz."

## Symbolic Meanings

### Primary Meanings:
- **Physical strength and vitality**
- **Primal, untamed energy**
- **Health and endurance**
- **Courage and tenacity**
- **Masculine/raw life force**
- **Formation and manifestation**

### In Divination:
- **Upright:** Strength to overcome obstacles, good health, new beginnings formed through sheer will, opportunity requiring courage, physical or mental fortitude
- **Reversed (Merkstave):** Weakness, missed opportunities, domination by others, illness, misdirected force, brutality without wisdom

### Spiritual Significance:
Uruz teaches us about the power of raw potential. It represents:
- The wild, untamed aspects of our nature
- The primal life force that drives us
- Strength that must be channeled wisely
- The power to shape our reality through will
- The importance of maintaining physical vitality

Unlike Fehu's mobile wealth, Uruz is about personal power—the internal strength that cannot be taken away.

## Historical Usage

Uruz appears in numerous Viking Age contexts:
- **Healing charms** for physical strength and recovery
- **Warrior talismans** for courage in battle
- **Hunting rituals** before dangerous expeditions
- **Coming-of-age ceremonies** marking transition to adulthood

The rune was often paired with Fehu—wealth (Fehu) and the strength to protect it (Uruz) formed a powerful combination.

## The Aurochs in Norse Culture

The aurochs held a special place in Germanic mythology:
- Its horns were prized as drinking vessels
- Hunting it demonstrated a man's worth
- It symbolized the wild, unconquered nature
- Its strength was seen as a gift from the gods

The famous drinking horns used in Norse feasts often came from domesticated cattle but were meant to evoke the mighty aurochs.

## Uruz and Personal Power

This rune reminds us that true strength comes from within. While Fehu represents external wealth, Uruz represents internal power—the kind that allows you to:
- Overcome obstacles through sheer determination
- Maintain health and vitality
- Stand firm in your convictions
- Shape your life through willpower

However, Uruz also warns against brutish force without wisdom. The aurochs was powerful but could be dangerous and destructive. Raw strength must be tempered with intelligence.

## Key Takeaways

1. Uruz represents primal strength and life force
2. It's named after the mighty aurochs, an extinct wild ox
3. The symbol teaches us about untamed personal power
4. It's pronounced "OO-rooz" and sounds like "oo" in "moon"
5. In Unicode, it's U+16A2
